Sound - Production & Transmission

Sound is produced when objects vibrate. These vibrations create alternating regions of compression (high pressure) and rarefaction (low pressure) in the surrounding medium.

Key Concept: Sound needs a medium (solid, liquid, or gas) to travel through. Sound CANNOT travel through a vacuum (empty space) because there are no particles to vibrate!

Sound in Different Media

Sound travels through solids, liquids, and gases - but the particles are packed differently in each. Click each medium to see how particles transmit vibrations.

Gas: Particles are far apart - sound travels slower (346 m/s in air)
Why does sound travel fastest in solids?
In solids, particles are closely packed and have strong bonds. When one particle vibrates, it quickly passes the vibration to its neighbor. In gases, particles are far apart and must travel farther to bump into the next particle.
